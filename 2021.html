PRIVATEPRIVEPUBLIC
The Spanish National Health System (SNS) is based in the principles of universality, free access, equity and fairness of financing, and is mainly funded by taxes.

I was insured with a private healthcare company by the firm that fired me(because the employees are supposed to be insured by their employers), which meant I could only go to doctors which cooperated with the private insurance company.

— —-this is where I found interesting, because people paid little trust to the government, they actually helped one another greatly.

Is healthcare a human right or self-responsibility in Spain?

Human rights, information, consent, confidentiality and privacy are well recognized in the Spanish legislation on patient rights since the General Act on Health 14/1986, where patients’ rights and duties were defined for the first time.

Since then, several laws have developed in-depth new rights on personal data protection (Law 15/1999), or patient’s autonomy (Law 41/2002).

Upon those principles, ACs have developed regional norms and executive decrees.

Why could Spain remain low-cost in healthcare after economic recession?

I didn’t get to find a public psychiatrist that spoke English, but thankfully the General Practitioners could prescribe my antidepressants and duloxetine in Barcelona.

Spain has the highest life-expectancy in EU of 83.49 years.

Its economic situation has been hampered greatly due to 2008 Recession.

The healthcare budget is thus shrinked greatly due to large unemployment rate.

The corresponding policy in 2012 has thus correspondingly shifts the entitlement of all citizens/residents to those who are currently under employment.

After 2018, the government has tried to go back to ‘the whole healthcare coverage for all citizens,’ especially for pandemic-related policies, at the cost of longer waiting time.

As from my limited experience in Spain, I didn’t feel that private hospitals outwin public ones, because the public hospitals are free. (or, like in any other European countries, healthcare is directly deducted from my salary) I actually felt very very secure in this land because people helped me automatically and understood my situation so well that I needn’t translate.

If there were one thing that I had to complain about the Spanish healthcare system, it was the prescribed medication prices.

Spain medication system aapted the Informal Referencing Pricing system, making comparison especially with France, Italy and Portugal, and set the medication prices lower than the lowest of the referencing prices.

But to my personal experiences, the prescribed medications were the same prices as other countries, as in Germany or Switzerland.

PRIVACY

I was in Barcelona from September 15th to December 15th 2021 until my Taiwanese tourist visa totally expired.

Spain has adapted BlueCard system — — i.e., non-European citizens with high qualified skills employed with a salary over certain threshold could apply for EU Blue Card as work permit, which allows them to work borderless inside EU zones.

In June 2021 I received a job offer as laser Engineer in Barcelona with the qualification of applying for EU Blue Card. By law, people could only work if, and only if, they have work permit in Spain.

But it took 8 months in average to obtain the work permit.

My employer suggested that we navigated around the law to enter Spain as if I were a self-employed consultant for a business trip.

‘Otherwise it takes too long to get you here,’said he.

I agreed, but the illegal routes they took was unsettling for me.

I went through custom pretending that I was a self-employed consultant.

On the 5th day I was fired due to internal managerial issues.

And here came the fascinating part — — I finally got to experience living as a tourist in Barcelona.

The day I was fired I found an apartment in the city center in Barcelona and moved in, determined to stay until the very last day my visa expired.

Parallelly, I had to resume my antidepressants and duloxetine dosage.

As usual, I had to find a psychiatrist to prescribe me.

So how easy was it to go see a doctor in Spain? More precisely, Barcelona?

When I went to random hospitals and told them that I was fired so I was no longer insured by my former employers, the doctors actually saw me for free and gave me free prescriptions.

The other day I fell off my bunk bed with my head hitting the floor directly — — I went to the emergencies at the nearest hospital.

I didn’t speak Spanish, so it was a passersby that helped me translate to the doctors and receptionist.

The passersby girl is herself a first-year medical student.

‘I just wanted to help, ’said she.

The receptionist in the hospital insisted to register me as an emergency case even if my health card didn’t function anymore.

She sent me to the emergency doctors directly.

Afterwards it was like in German emergencies — — lengthy waiting times, lengthy examinations, thorough MRI scan, until midnight I received my medical report— — -for free.

It took me five hours to go through whole examinations in the hospital.

But I couldn’t complain.
